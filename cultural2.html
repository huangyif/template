Introduction

The internet is doubtless one of the greatest inventions in human history. In August 1962, the concept of “Galactic Network” was propounded by J.C.R. Licklider, a computer scientist at Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Like James Watts’ invention of the steam engine, which prompted communications between countries on different continents, the internet has revolutionized almost every field of industry. With the rapid development of communications technology, the internet has become an important part of work and life.

In fact, almost everything in people’s lives is now connected to the internet. A typical college student’s life is a good example of this phenomena. At universities, the internet is used to make education more efficient. Student course materials can be distributed and organized much more easily. Submission of student projects or homework need not be restricted to class hours. If a student is busy with his or her work, he or she can take online courses at home or anywhere else at a more comfortable time.

For a college student, time spent on social networks like Facebook makes up the largest part of his or her time spent on the Internet. According to statistics reported by Facebook, there are 1.1 billion users who visit Facebook actively each month. 48 percent of 18-34 year olds check Facebook when they wake up, and people in that demographic spend, on average, 20 minutes on Facebook each visit. In fact, social networks like Facebook, Google Plus, and Twitter change people’s lifestyles enormously. An active Facebook user’s rough autobiography can easily be assembled solely by visiting his profile site. Making friends is also much easier than ever before. By clicking on the request a friend button, one can even make friends who do not speak the same language.
